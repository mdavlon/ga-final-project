Bowie's Berlin

Adapted from David Bowie's London, Berlin and New York blog post for lastminute.com/Spotify.

Best viewed in desktop mode

After a brief, but troubled (and drug-addled) period living in LA. Bowie moved to Berlin in 1976, along with his friend, former Stooges-frontman Iggy Pop.

Inspired by its architecture and history (and it also helped that the city was very cheap) Bowie had a long-standing fascination with the city. And the period Bowie spent there became one of the most productive in his career, inspiring not just his own “Berlin Trilogy” – the classic albums Low, Heroes and Lodger – but also Pop’s The Idiot and Lust for Life.
